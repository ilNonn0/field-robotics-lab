01 — Autonomous Exploration & SLAM
Navigate the unknown,
map as you go
We develop SLAM algorithms and autonomous exploration strategies for unknown environments — indoor and outdoor, GPS-denied — with a focus on target-oriented search for Search and Rescue (SaR) missions.
+
Scientific highlights

Active exploration combines SLAM with navigation strategies where the robot autonomously decides where to move to maximise information gain. Current directions include Semantic SLAM (object-labelled maps), Lifelong SLAM (dynamic environment adaptation), Deep SLAM (neural visual representations), and Multi-Agent Cooperative SLAM (C-SLAM) for collaborative map building. Key challenges: temporal efficiency, map completeness, target localisation, and onboard computational constraints.

Practical applications

Search and rescue in unknown 3D environments; autonomous indoor exploration with reduced coverage time; UAV, UGV, and AUV operations in GPS-denied areas; augmented reality with precise spatial tracking.

02 — Heterogeneous Multi-Agent Cooperation
UAV meets UGV,
missions multiply
We study the coordination of autonomous agents with different structures and capabilities — combining aerial and ground robots to collaboratively achieve complex missions that neither could accomplish alone.
+
Scientific highlights

Key research directions include Multi-Agent Reinforcement Learning (MARL) for decentralised sequential decision-making, and Vision-Language Models (VLMs) for fine-grained visual reasoning in cooperative tasks. Open challenges remain in heterogeneous cooperation, particularly in scenarios requiring detailed visual understanding and emergent compositional cooperation patterns.

Practical applications

Hierarchical task planning across heterogeneous agents; multi-objective optimisation for collaborative task allocation; joint aerial-ground exploration for search and rescue; GNSS-denied cooperative localisation — validated at the Leonardo Drone Contest 2023 (1st place).

Related publications
  • M. Mugnai, M. Satler, and C. A. Avizzano, "Heterogeneous Multi-robot Systems Cooperative Exploration of Unknown Environment," in European Robotics Forum, Springer, 2025.

03 — Infrastructure Inspection
Into confined spaces,
beyond human reach
We deploy autonomous UAVs for inspection of bridges, buildings, industrial plants, and confined spaces — detecting structural defects automatically, without exposing operators to hazardous environments. A landmark activity is the inspection of an ENEL boiler using a custom micro aerial vehicle.
+
Scientific highlights

Research integrates AI-enhanced drone methodology with consumer-grade cameras and open-source software for automated defect detection. Deep learning enables UAV-based visual inspection with neural networks trained for anomaly recognition. For 3D reconstruction, we deploy FastLIVO2 — a state-of-the-art LiDAR-Inertial-Visual odometry system — onboard our drone platforms, enabling dense real-time mapping of complex structures.

Practical applications

Inspection of bridges, dams, and buildings in remote or hazardous locations; industrial plant inspection in boilers and confined spaces; 3D structural reconstruction for digital twin generation; critical infrastructure monitoring without human risk exposure.

Related publications
  • P. Tripicchio, M. Satler, M. Unetti, and C. A. Avizzano, "Confined Spaces Industrial Inspection with Micro Aerial Vehicles and Laser Range Finder Localization," Int. J. Micro Air Veh., vol. 10, no. 2, 2018.
  • M. Satler, M. Unetti, N. Giordani, C. A. Avizzano, and P. Tripicchio, "Towards an Autonomous Flying Robot for Inspections in Open and Constrained Spaces," in Proc. IEEE SSD, 2014.

04 — Environmental Monitoring
Wide-scale sensing,
where humans cannot go
We develop aerial and ground robotic systems for large-scale periodic monitoring of natural and agricultural environments — collecting environmental data in areas that are remote, hazardous, or impractical to survey manually.
+
Scientific highlights

Research spans remote sensing with UAV platforms equipped with multispectral, thermal, and LiDAR sensors; autonomous data collection pipelines for precision agriculture; and photogrammetry-based territorial mapping for environmental change assessment. We also explore autonomous aerial systems in complex cultivation environments — woodland and orchard settings — where GPS coverage is limited and canopy structure requires adaptive flight strategies.

Practical applications

Precision agriculture and crop monitoring with farm robotics; mapping and assessment of remote or contaminated areas; photogrammetric territorial surveys; environmental monitoring in the context of the national Agritech PNRR partnership.

Related publications
  • P. Tripicchio, M. Satler, G. Dabisias, E. Ruffaldi, and C. A. Avizzano, "Towards Smart Farming and Sustainable Agriculture with Drones," in Proc. Intell. Environ., 2015.

Join the lab

Work with us on autonomous robotics

We are always looking for motivated students and researchers who want to work on real autonomous systems — not just simulations. Our lab offers hands-on experience with custom UAVs, ground robots, and state-of-the-art sensing in a well-equipped indoor arena.

We work at the intersection of aerial robotics, SLAM, multi-agent systems, and edge computing. If you are passionate about making robots fly, navigate, and cooperate autonomously, we want to hear from you.

We welcome applications from Master's students (thesis projects), PhD candidates, and visiting researchers. Experience with ROS 2, PX4, Python, or C++ is a plus — curiosity and rigour are essential.
